Why Lehigh County Roofs Need Special Attention

❄️

Extreme Weather Cycles

Lehigh County experiences over 100 freeze-thaw cycles annually, creating the perfect conditions for ice dams - the #1 cause of roof damage in our area.

🌡️

Temperature Extremes

With winter temperatures dropping to 22°F and summer highs reaching 86°F, your roof endures a 64-degree temperature swing that tests every component.

🌧️

Heavy Precipitation

Our region receives 44 inches of precipitation yearly, with recent data showing a 37% increase in extreme weather events since 2000.

Seasonal Roofing Concerns

Summer Storm Damage

  • Intense thunderstorms with damaging winds
  • Hail events causing shingle damage
  • Torrential downpours overwhelming gutters
  • UV exposure accelerating material degradation

Winter Weather Challenges

  • Heavy snow loads up to 30 pounds per square foot
  • Ice dam formation from temperature fluctuations
  • Freeze-thaw cycles causing material expansion
  • Wind-driven snow infiltration

Spring & Fall Concerns

  • Spring reveals winter damage requiring repair
  • Fall leaf accumulation blocking drainage
  • Temperature swings stressing roofing materials
  • Increased moisture promoting algae growth

Current Code Requirements

2018 Pennsylvania Uniform Construction Code

  • Wind resistance: 90 mph minimum
  • Attic insulation: R-38 minimum (R-49 recommended)
