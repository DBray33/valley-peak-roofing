7 Tips to Choose the Best Roofing Contractor in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania

July 9, 2025 Valley Peak Roofing Team Roofing Tips

Roofing in Bethlehem, PA, is one of the most valuable assets of a house. A properly functioning roof protects your home from the elements and keeps everyone inside safe. To help ensure that your roof stays in mint condition year-round, we’ve put together seven tips to consider when choosing a roofing contractor in Bethlehem, PA.

1. Licensing

Pennsylvania requires all roofers in Bethlehem, PA, to hold a valid license before starting work on someone’s property. The best roofing in Bethlehem, PA, will always abide by local building codes to prevent issues for themselves or the homeowner. Before scheduling roof repair, always ensure the contractor has a valid license.

2. Insurance

Roofing is a dangerous task. This is why homeowners call in the experts for repairs or replacements. Roofers in Bethlehem, PA, work at great heights and use appropriate PPE. A minor mishap can lead to serious injury. As a homeowner, you need to confirm the contractor is insured before hiring them — otherwise, you may face financial or legal liability if they get injured on your property. The best roofers will always have the appropriate licensing and insurance.

3. Workmanship

To guarantee the best roofing in Bethlehem, PA, look at the contractor’s portfolio. This should include photos of previous work. They may even share nearby properties they’ve worked on. Assessing their expertise and workmanship will help you choose the right one for your project.

4. Experience

Roof repair in Bethlehem, PA, should always be conducted by an experienced contractor. How long has the company been in business? Are they accredited by the Better Business Bureau? Always go with an experienced roofer to ensure the job is done right the first time.

5. Cost

When shopping around for a roofing contractor in Bethlehem, PA, the cost is likely at the top of your list. Don’t compromise the quality of materials or workmanship to save a few dollars. Reputable roofers will always offer a fair price, experience, and high-quality workmanship.

6. Warranty

A new roof is a large investment that can last for decades with proper maintenance. However, if the material turns out to be defective, a warranty can help offset the cost of repairs. Ask about warranties on both materials and labor. Working with a licensed contractor minimizes the risk of voiding the manufacturer’s warranty and gives you greater value for your money.

7. Reputation

What are nearby residents saying about your roofing contractor? Are they trusted by the community? Check online review sites and the contractor’s website to read client testimonials. Avoid roofers with several negative reviews and complaints.

Final Thoughts

Don’t risk the safety of your home by hiring an inexperienced roofer. You need a contractor that can guarantee a job well done the first time around. Look for one that’s experienced, licensed, insured, and has a good reputation — you can’t go wrong!
