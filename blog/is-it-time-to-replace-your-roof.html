Is It Time to Replace Your Roof?

March 15, 2024 Valley Peak Roofing Team Roofing Tips

5 Signs Pennsylvania Homeowners Shouldn't Ignore

Your roof protects everything you love — your family, your memories, and the home you've worked so hard for. But like anything exposed to the elements year after year, even the strongest roof can wear down over time.

Living in Bethlehem, PA, where our weather brings everything from snowy winters to summer storms, it's especially important to know when your roof is trying to tell you it's time for a change.

Here are five signs your roof might need replacing — and why now is the right time to act.

1. Your Shingles Are Curling, Cracked, or Missing

Shingles are your roof's first line of defense. If they're curling at the edges, cracking, or falling off entirely, it means they're past their prime. This is especially common after years of freeze-thaw cycles and windstorms common in the Lehigh Valley.

What it means: Water can easily find its way beneath damaged shingles, leading to rot, leaks, or mold.

2. You See Dark Streaks or Moss Growing

Those black streaks on your roof aren't just dirt — they're often algae. Moss growth traps moisture against your shingles, accelerating damage.

Bethlehem weather tip: Humid springs and shady areas create the perfect conditions for moss and algae.

What it means: A deeper problem could be lurking under the surface.

3. There's Visible Sagging

A sagging roofline is a serious red flag. It can mean structural damage, often from trapped moisture, poor ventilation, or long-term water intrusion.

What it means: Don't wait. This could lead to a partial collapse or costly framing repairs.

4. You're Finding Granules in Your Gutters

If your gutters are full of shingle granules (those tiny sand-like particles), your roof is probably wearing out. These granules help protect your shingles from the sun's UV rays and add weight to resist wind.

What it means: Your shingles are breaking down and may no longer protect your home effectively.

5. Your Roof Is Over 20 Years Old

Even if it looks okay, a roof that's 20–25 years old has likely outlived its warranty and weathered plenty of storms. It's often better (and cheaper) to replace before serious issues develop.

What it means: An inspection now could save you thousands in emergency repairs later.
