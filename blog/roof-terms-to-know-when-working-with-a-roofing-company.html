Roof “Terms” to Know When Working with a Roofing Company

July 9, 2025 Valley Peak Roofing Team Roofing Tips

When working with a roofing contractor in Bethlehem, PA, it can sometimes feel like you’re speaking two different languages. But don’t fret — just knowing a few key roofing terms can help you bridge the gap when you need roof repair in Bethlehem, PA! Read on to learn the most important terms in roofing.

Decking

Roof decking refers to the boards that form the “base” your shingles or other roofing material rest upon. While wood is a popular choice for the best roofing in Bethlehem, PA, decking can also be steel or concrete. The decking can be damaged even when shingles look fine. Watch for sagging rooflines, holes, or mildew.

Drip Edge

The drip edge sits at the edge of your roof. Its L-shape helps deflect water away from shingles and fascia — it’s one of the most important parts of affordable roofing in Bethlehem, PA. In the Northeast, drip edges are a must for keeping water, snow, and ice from damaging your roof.

Gable

A gable is a triangle-shaped section of roofing, often above windows or projecting out from the main roof. Gabled styles are very popular in the Northeast — roofers in Bethlehem, PA, should be experienced with reroofing gabled homes.

Fascia

A fascia is a long board that sits under the roof edge and behind a home’s rain gutters. It provides a stable base for gutters and protects against moisture. Damaged fascia calls for immediate roof repair in Bethlehem, PA, as it can lead to mold growth and structural issues.

Flashing

Flashing refers to small plates, usually metal, that deflect water away from critical areas like chimneys, vents, and skylights. Flashing is vital to preventing leaks. The best roofing in Bethlehem, PA, will include strong, properly installed flashing to handle heavy rain and snow.

Hip

A hip refers to the external angle formed where two roof slopes meet. Think of a roof with all sides sloping downward — where they meet at corners, those are hips. Roofers in Bethlehem, PA, know that damaged hips put the entire roof at risk of leaks.

Ridge

A ridge is where two planes of the roof meet along the highest horizontal line. It needs to be well protected to prevent leaks and ensure proper water runoff. A good roofing contractor in Bethlehem, PA, will take extra care with ridge construction and protection.

Soffit

Soffit refers to the underside surface beneath an overhanging section of roof (or eave). It protects rafters from sun and rain, and it’s often used as a design element. A well-made soffit can add a decorative touch while enhancing roof durability.

Know What You’re Buying

Understanding roofing terms doesn’t just help you speak confidently with your roofer — it ensures you understand what you’re investing in and how your roof protects your home. Keep this glossary on hand and book affordable roofing in Bethlehem, PA, with confidence!
