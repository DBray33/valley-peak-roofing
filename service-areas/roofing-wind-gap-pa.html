Wind Gap's Weather Challenges

100+ Freeze-Thaw Cycles

January averages 35°F/22°F while July reaches 82°F/64°F. With over 100 annual freeze-thaw cycles, material expansion and contraction creates significant stress, compromising roofing integrity throughout the year.

Wind Tunnel Effect

The Blue Mountain gap creates natural wind tunnel amplifying storms. February brings 6.6 mph average winds, but severe events reach 53-90 mph. The gap effect increases exposure to all weather systems.

May 6, 2025: EF-1 tornado nearby, 80-90 mph. February 15-16: 9-16 inches snow, 53 mph gusts. Hurricane Debby: 2-4 inches rain, widespread flooding.
